Japanese-Style Chicken Curry

A savory & hearty curry guaranteed to keep your belly full on frigid nights. This is a recipe I found on Pinterest & have made for years, with modifications - have fun with it!

Ingredients

  • 6 Chicken Thighs; boneless + skinless
  • 2 Medium Sliced Carrots
  • 4 Diced Garlic Cloves; or as many as your heart desires
  • 1 Large Onion, Halved & Diced.
  • 2 Large Diced Potatoes; peeled or unpeeled.
  • 1 TBSP Honey
  • 2 TBSP Soy Sauce
  • 2 TBSP Curry Powder; mild, or spicy if you're bold
  • 2 TBSP Flour, sifted
  • 1 TSP Garam Masala
  • 2 Cups Chicken Stock
  • 2 TBSP Vegetable Oil; the recipe referenced calls for Veg. but I use Olive or Avocado oils
  • Rice to serve; rinsed

This recipe takes approximately 1 hour to prepare & complete, yielding 6 servings.


Here's How to Make It:

  1. Heat oil until fragrant in a medium saucepan; add Chicken.
  2. Cook chicken until browned completely; set aside & deglaze pan by adding more oil; use Medium heat.
  3. Add Onions & Garlic; cook until softened, around 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add Flour, Curry Powder & Garam Masala. Cook for 1-2 minutes, then gradually add the Chicken Stock.
  5. Once combined, add Honey & Soy Sauce
  6. Add previously browned Chicken, Carrots, & Potatoes.
  7. Slowly bring to an even boil & simmer for 15-20 minutes.

    8. **Please see below for modified InstantPot recipe**

    InstantPot Instructions


    1. Using the Saute function set for 12 mins, heat oil in conductive bowl & brown Chicken on all sides; set aside.
    2. Deglaze bowl with additional oil, scrape and toss any burnt pieces. Replace oil & set Saute function to 5 mins, adding Garlic & Onions. Set aside once softened.
    3. Once again, set Saute for 10 minutes & add Chicken Stock, Curry Powder & Garam Masala, stirring occassionally. Once warmed, add Potatoes & Carrots. Cook until function times out.
    4. Add browned chicken, onions, garlic, Honey, & Soy Sauce. Stir until combined & affix lid to 'Sealing' position.
    5. Set Pressure to high for 12 mins, allow cooker to release pressure for 10 mins. Serve with Rice.
      6. If your curry comes out watery, add 2 TBSP Corn Starch dissolved in water, this should thicken it up nicely.

    This is my favorite recipe as curry is typically a universally loved dish, but it also is easy to cook while leaving plenty for leftovers. Soups are definitely a staple food in a home with working owners!

    Not only is this recipe easy, but it also is very friendly to alterations. Adding other vegetables, such as bell peppers, or spices such as cardamom or ginger, can really make it a show-stopper.
